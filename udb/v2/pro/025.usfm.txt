\c 25
\s1 More wise sayings from Solomon
\p
\v 1 Here are more wise sayings that Solomon wrote. Some men who worked for Hezekiah, the king of Judah, copied them from a scroll that Solomon had written.
\b
\q1
\v 2 We consider God to be great because he acts in mysterious ways;
\q2 we consider kings to be great because they explain things.
\b
\q1
\v 3 It is not possible for anyone to measure how high the sky is or how deep the earth is;
\q2 likewise , it is not possible for us to know all that kings are thinking.
\b
\q1
\v 4 If workers burn out the impure bits that are in silver,
\q2 a man who makes things from silver can make something beautiful from the silver.
\q1
\v 5 Similarly, if wicked advisors are taken away from a king,
\q2 his government will remain secure, because the king will be able to act justly.
\b
\q1
\v 6 When you stand in front of a king, do not try to impress him
\q2 and do not act like you are important
\q1
\v 7 it is better if someone tells you to sit closer to the king
\q2 than for someone to tell you, while the king is listening, to sit further away in order that someone who is more important may sit closer to the king.
\b
\q1
\v 8 Do not quickly go to a court to tell the judge about something that you have seen,
\q2 because another witness may later say something that proves that you are wrong, and as a result you will be disgraced.
\q2 If that happens, what will you do?
\b
\q1
\v 9 If you and someone else think differently about some matter,
\q2 settle it between yourselves, and do not tell others any secret that he has told you.
\q1
\v 10 If others find out that you have done that,
\q2 you will be ashamed,
\q2 and from that time on, you will have a bad reputation.
\b
\q1
\v 11 Something that is said that is very appropriate
\q2 is as delightful as seeing  in a silver bowl.
\b
\q1
\v 12 When a wise person rebukes who is willing to listen,
\q2 that is as valuable as  a gold ring or a gold chain.
\b
\q1
\v 13 A messenger who is reliable refreshes the spirits of his bosses who sent him
\q2 like  refreshes the ground at the time that people harvest crops.
\b
\q1
\v 14 When someone promises to give a gift to us but never gives it,
\q2 that disappoints us as much as  clouds and wind that come but do not bring any rain.
\b
\q1
\v 15 If someone keeps requesting a ruler long enough to do something, he will often agree to do it;
\q2 similarly, by speaking  gently we can often convince others that what we say is right [IDI].
\b
\q1
\v 16 If you find some honey, do not eat a lot of it,
\q2 because doing that may cause you to vomit.
\q1
\v 17 Do not go to your neighbor's house very often to talk with him;
\q2 if you go very often, he will get tired of listening to you and start to hate you.
\b
\q1
\v 18 To falsely accuse others in court
\q2 is like  attacking them with a war-club or a sword or a sharp arrow.
\b
\q1
\v 19 Depending on unreliable people when you have troubles
\q2 is as bad as trying to eat when you have a bad tooth or trying to walk when your foot is crippled.
\b
\q1
\v 20 Singing to someone who is depressed just causes him to feel worse;
\q2 it is like  taking off clothes on a very cold day
\q2 or like putting vinegar on a wound.
\b
\q1
\v 21 If your enemies are hungry, give them something to eat;
\q2 if they are thirsty, give them something to drink;
\q1
\v 22 doing that will cause them to feel ashamed [IDI],
\q2 and Yahweh will reward you for doing that.
\b
\q1
\v 23 When wind blows from the north, it will rain;
\q2 similarly  about others, that causes them to look at us very angrily.
\b
\q1
\v 24 It is better to live alone in the corner of an attic, Or: housetop
\q2 than to live inside the house with a wife who is always nagging.
\b
\q1
\v 25 Receiving good news from a country far away refreshes our spirits
\q2 like  cold water refreshes our bodies when we are very thirsty.
\b
\q1
\v 26 When a righteous to wicked people,
\q2 that is as bad as  a spring that becomes muddied or a fountain that becomes polluted.
\b
\q1
\v 27 It is not good to eat too much honey,
\q2 and trying to get people to praise you is also not good.
\b
\q1
\v 28 People who cannot control their tempers are unable to defend their behavior;
\q2 that is like having a city without a wall around it, with the result that no one can defend it.
