====== Sample Metaphor Notes ======

Sample notes from Jim's revisions:

  * **For just as the sufferings of Christ abound for our sake** - Paul speaks of Christ's sufferings as if they were objects that could increase in number. AT: "For just as Christ suffered greatly for our sake"  (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]])
  * **our comfort abounds** - Paul speaks of comfort as if it were an object that could increase in size (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]])
  * **abound in glory** - Paul speaks of the work that Christ did as if it were an object that could produce another object. He means that Christ's service of righteousness is far more glorious than the law, which also had glory. (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]])
  * **unveiled faces, see the glory of the Lord** - Unveiling a person's face allows him to see clearly and also refers to being able to understand. The word "seeing" refers to the ability to understand something. (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]])
  * **inheritance** - The Christian believers are presented here as God's own people. They are spoken of as if they were something that he inherited. (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]]) 
  * **You must put off...the old man** - Paul is speaking of moral qualities as if they were pieces of clothing. AT: "You must change your behavior." (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]]) 
  * **strangers to the covenants of the promise** - Paul speaks to the Gentile believers as if they had been foreigners, kept out of the land of God's covenant and promise. (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]])
  * **you were dead in your trespasses and sins** - This shows how sinful people are unable to obey God in the same way a dead person is unable to respond physically. (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]])
  * **children of wrath** - people with whom God is angry (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]])
  * **one new man** - A single new people, the people of redeemed humanity. (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]])
  * **You have been built on the foundation** - Paul speaks of God's people as if they were a building. Christ is the cornerstone, the apostles are the foundation, and the believers are the structure. (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]])
  * **the whole building fits together and grows as a temple** - Paul continues with the comparison of Christ's family to a building. In the same way a builder fits stones together while building, so Christ is fitting us together. (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]])
  * **the many-sided nature of the wisdom of God** - God's complex wisdom (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]])
  * **heirs** - The people to whom God has made promises are spoken of as if they were to inherit property and wealth from a family member. (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]]) 
  * **hold tightly to the confession** - Here a set of beliefs is spoken of as if it were an object that a person could hold onto. (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]])
  * **wavering** - An  uncertain about something is spoken of as if he were wavering or leaning from side to side. AT: "being unsure" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]])
  * **the day approaching** - A future time is spoken of as if it were an object coming closer to the speaker. (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]])
  * ** a fury of fire that will consume God's enemies** - God's fury is spoken of as if it were fire that would burn up his enemies. (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]])
  * **trampled on the Son of God** - Disregarding Christ and scorning him are spoken of as if someone had walked over him. (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]])
  * **Vengeance belongs to me** - Vengeance is spoken of as if it were an object that belonged to God, who had the right to do as he wished with what he owned. God has the right to take vengeance on his enemies. (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]])
  * **I will pay back** - God taking vengeance is spoken of as if he were paying someone his wages. (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]])
  * **fall into the hands** - This phrase compares God's punishment with being in his powerful and just hands. AT: "be under the full judgment." (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]])
  * **a better and everlasting possession** - God's eternal blessings are spoken of as a "possession." (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]])
  * **throw away** - Rejecting a promise is spoken of as if it were throwing away something worthless or useless. (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]])
  * **If he turns back** - Losing courage and faith are spoken of as if it were turning back from following a path. (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]])
  * **keeping our soul** - Living eternally with God is spoken of as if it were keeping one's soul. (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]])
