====== sin, sinful, sinner ======


===== Definition: =====

The term "sin" refers to actions, thoughts, and words that are against God's will and laws.

    * People, desires, thoughts and behaviors that disobey God's will are called "sinful."
    * A "sinner" is someone who sins, so this applies to every human being.
    * Sin can also refer to not doing something that God wants us to do.
    * Sin includes anything we do that does not obey or please God, whether other people know about it or not.
    * Because Adam sinned, all human beings are born with a sinful nature. The sinful nature is passed from parent to child through the entire human race.
    * Sometimes the word "sinner" was used by religious people like the Pharisees to refer to people they considered to be worse sinners than others. For example, this label was given to tax collectors and prostitutes.

===== Translation Suggestions: =====

    * "To sin" could also be translated as "to disobey God" or "to do wrong."
    * "Sinful" could also be translated as "wicked" or "immoral" or "evil."
    * The term "sinner" could be translated with a word or phrase that means, "person who sins" or "evil person" or "person who disobeys God."
    * The figurative use of "sinner" could be translated by a word or phrase that means, "very sinful people" or "people considered to be very sinful" or "immoral people."
    * Ways to translate the phrase, "tax collectors and sinners" could include, "people who collect money for the government, and other very sinful people."
