====== bless ======


==== (bless, blessed, blessing) ====


===== Definition: =====

To "bless" someone or something is to state a desire for positive and beneficial things to happen to them. In Bible times a father would often pronounce a formal blessing on his children.

  * To bless something or someone means to cause good and beneficial things to happen to the person or thing that is being blessed.
  * When people 'bless" God, this means they are praising him.
  * To "bless" could also be translated as, "to provide abundantly for" or "to be very kind and favorable toward."
  * "God has brought great blessing to" could be translated as, "God has given many good things to" or "God has provided abundantly for" or "God has caused many good things to happen to".
  * "He is blessed" could be translated as "he will greatly benefit" or "he will experience good things" or "God will cause him to flourish."
  * "Blessed is the person who" could be translated as "How good it is for the person who"
  * When it says that Jesus "blessed" food, this could be translated as "praised" or "thanked" God for the food.

  ***[[:en:obs:notes:frames:04-07|[04-07]]]** Melchizedek **blessed** Abram and said, "May God Most High who owns heaven and earth **bless** Abram."
  ***[[:en:obs:notes:frames:07-03|[07-03]]]** Isaac wanted to give his **blessing** to Esau.
  ***[[:en:obs:notes:frames:08-05|[08-05]]]** Even in prison, Joseph remained faithful to God, and God **blessed** him.
