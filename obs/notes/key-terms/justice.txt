====== justice ======


==== (justice, justly) ====


===== Definition: =====

Justice is the fair and right treatment of all people under the laws of God and country.

  * To act "justly" means to treat people in a way that is right, good and proper according to God's laws.
  * To receive justice means to be treated fairly under the law, either being protected by law or receiving due punishment for breaking the law.


[See also: **[[en:obs:notes:key-terms:judge|judge]]**]


===== Examples from the Bible stories: =====

  ***[[:en:obs:notes:frames:17-09|[17-09]]]** David ruled with **justice** and faithfulness for many years, and God blessed him.
  ***[[:en:obs:notes:frames:18-13|[18-13]]]** Some of these kings (of Judah) were good men who ruled **justly** and worshiped God.
  ***[[:en:obs:notes:frames:19-16|[19-16]]]** They (the prophets) all told the people to stop worshiping idols and to start showing **justice** and mercy to others.
  ***[[:en:obs:notes:frames:50-17|[50-17]]]** Jesus will rule his kingdom with peace and **justice**, and he will be with his people forever.
