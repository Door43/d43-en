====== glory, glorious ======

===== Definition: =====

In general, the term "glory" means honor, praise and splendor; it also can refer to extreme greatness. Anything that has glory is said to be "glorious."

  * In the Old Testament, the word for glory has the idea of something being weighty, unchanging, and valuable.
  * In the New Testament, the word for glory communicates splendor, brightness, and judgment.
  * The term glory is especially used to describe God who is more glorious than anyone or anything in the universe. Everything in his character reveals his glory and his splendor.
  * To "give God glory" is to honor, praise, and worship him. It is also an idiomatic way of urging someone to tell the truth about something.
  * When we say that something brings God glory, it means that it leads people to honor, praise, and worship him.


===== Examples from the Bible stories: =====

  ***[[:en:obs:notes:frames:23-07|[23-07]]]** Suddenly, the skies were filled with angels praising God, saying, "**Glory** to God in heaven and peace on earth to the people he favors!"
  ***[[:en:obs:notes:frames:25-06|[25-06]]]** Then Satan showed Jesus all the kingdoms of the world and all their **glory** and said, "I will give you all this if you bow down and worship me."
  ***[[:en:obs:notes:frames:37-01|[37-01]]]** When Jesus heard this news, he said, "This sickness will not end in death, but it is for the **glory** of God."
  ***[[:en:obs:notes:frames:37-08|[37-08]]]** Jesus responded, "Did I not tell you that you would see God's **glory** if you believe in me?"
