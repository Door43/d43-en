====== Important Information about English Grammar ======


===== Active or Passive =====


The English language has the active and passive form of speech.


    * In the ACTIVE form, the subject does the action.
    * In the PASSIVE form, the action is done to the subject.


Go to this [[https://door43.org/en/ta/vocabulary/active|page]] for more information.


===== Exclusive =====


Some languages have two sets of pronouns for “we” “us” and “our”. The exclusive pronouns refer only to the speaker and at least one other person, but not the person being spoken to. They exclude the person being spoken to.


Go to this [[https://door43.org/en/ta/vocabulary/exclusive|page]] or watch a video for the [[http://youtu.be/W6m0Wi5y9Po|computer]] or the [[http://youtu.be/4oAnEMimBXE|tablet/phone]] for more information.


===== Inclusive =====


Some languages have two sets of pronouns for “we” “us” and “our”. The inclusive pronouns refer to the speaker and the person or people he is talking to, and possibly others as well. These pronouns are called “inclusive” because, in addition to the speaker, they include the person being spoken to.”


Go to this [[https://door43.org/en/ta/vocabulary/inclusive|page]] for more information.

=== Plural Pronouns ===


Plural pronouns replace plural nouns — those that name more than one person, place, thing, or idea.

Singular pronouns replace singular nouns, which are those that name one person, place, thing, or idea.

===== Speech Quotations =====


In English there are direct or non-direct, or indirect, quotations.


    * DIRECT quotations are when the words that were said originally are used. (She said, “Come.” and then he said, “I will come.”)
    * INDIRECT or NON-DIRECT quotations are when the original words are changed. (She told him to come. He said he would come.) The quote marks ” ” are not used and often there is a change in the verb tense.
