===== Litotes =====

**This page answers the question:** //What are litotes?//

In order to understand this topic, it would be good to read:
  * //[[en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_intro|Figures of Speech]]//

Litotes is a strong statement made by negative phrases to strengthen a positive meaning.  Litotes are made to say that the opposite idea is not true.
===== Translation Suggestions =====
  * Use the litotes if it would be understood.
  * If it would not be clear, say emphatically what __is__  true.
===== Examples from the Bible =====
**For you yourselves know, brothers, our visit to you was __not in vain__,** 1 Thessalonians 2:1\\
By using litotes, Paul emphasized that his visit with them was very worthwhile.\\ 
\\ 
**All things were made through him. __Without him__ was __not anything__ made that has been made.** John 1:3 (WEB)\\
By using litotes John emphasized that the Son of God created absolutely everything.
