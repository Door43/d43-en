====== Psalms Chapter 083 ======

===== Comprehension Questions and Answers =====

These questions will be used by translators to conduct community checks of this chapter.

Q? Who is the writer asking to not be silent, not ignore them and remain unmoved?

A. The writer is asking God. [83:1]

Q? What does the writer say about the enemies of God and those who hate God?

A. They are making a commotion and have raised their heads. [83:2]

Q? Who do the enemies of God conspire against and plan against?

A. They conspire against God's people and plan against God's protected ones. [83:3]

Q? What do God's enemies say they will do to Israel?

A. They said, "let us destroy them as a nation." [83:4]

Q? What would happen to the name of Israel?

A. Israel would no longer be remembered. [83:]

Q? Against whom have God's enemies schemed?

A. They schemed against God and made an alliance. [83:5]

Q? Who are included in the alliance that God's enemies have made?

A. It included the tents of Edom, the Ishmaelites, the people of Moab, and the Hagrites who plot together with Gebal, Ammon, Amalek and includes Philistia and the inhabitants of Tyre. [83:6-7]

Q? What other country is allied with God's enemies and who are they helping?

A. Assyria is allied with them and they are helping the descendants of Lot. [83:8]

Q? What is the writer asking God to do to his enemies as he did to Midian, Sisera and Jabin?

A. He is asking that they perish at Endor and become like manure for the earth. [83:9-10]

Q? What did their nobles like Oreb and Zeeb and their princes like Zebah and Zalmunna say?

A. They said, "Let us take for ourselves the pastures of God." [83:11-12]

Q? What things does the writer want God to make his enemies like?

A. He wants God to make them like whirling dust, chaff before the wind, fire that burns the forest and the flame that sets the mountains on fire. [83:13-14]

Q? How does the writer want God to chase and terrify his enemies?

A. He wants God to chase them with God's strong wind and terrify them with God's windstorm. [83:15]

Q? Why does the writer ask Yahweh to fill the enemies faces with shame?

A. He asks so that the enemies might seek Yahweh's name. [83:16]

Q? What does the writer desire would happen to God's enemies?

A. He desires that they be put to shame, be terrified forever and perish in disgrace. [83:17]

Q? What will God's enemies know about Yahweh?

A. They will know that Yahweh alone is the Most High over all the earth. [83:]
