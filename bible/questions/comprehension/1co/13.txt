====== 1 Corinthians Chapter 13 ======

===== Comprehension Questions and Answers =====

These questions will be used by translators to conduct community checks of this chapter.

Q? What do you become if you speak with tongues of men and of angels but don't have love?

A. You become a noisy gong or a clanging cymbal [13:1].

Q? What are you if you have the gift of prophecy, you understand all hidden truths and knowledge and have great faith, but don't have love?

A. You are nothing [13:2].

Q? How can you give all you own to feed the poor and give your body to be burned and still gain nothing?

A. If you don't have love you will gain nothing even though you do all these other things [13:3].

Q? What are some of the characteristics of love?

A. Love is patient and kind; it does not envy or boast; it isn't arrogant or rude. It is not self serving, not easily angered, nor does it keep a count of wrongs. It doesn't rejoice in unrighteousness but rejoices with the truth. It bears all things, believes all things, has confidence concerning all things, and endures all things. It never ends [13:4-8].

Q? What are some things that will pass away or cease?

A. Prophecies, knowledge and that which is incomplete will pass away and tongues will cease [13:8-10].

Q? What will never end?

A. Love never ends [13:8].

Q? What did Paul say he did when he became an adult?

A. Paul said when he became an adult, he put away childish things [13:11].

Q? What three things will remain and which of the three is the greatest?

A. Faith, confidence, and love will remain. The greatest of these is love [13:13].
