===== translationNotes: =====

  * **Let the sea shout and everything in it** - The psalmist speaks as if the sea were a person who could shout to God. AT: "Let it be as if the sea and everything in it shouts" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_personification]])
  * **the world and those who live in it** - The psalmist speaks as if the world were a person. AT: "and let the world and those who live in it shout" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_ellipsis]])
  * **Let the rivers clap their hands, and let the mountains shout for joy** - The psalmist speaks as if the rivers and mountains were people who could clap and shout. AT: "Let it be as though the rivers are clapping their hands and mountains were shouting for joy" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_personification]])
  * **the world ... the nations** - These phrases are metonyms for "the people in the world" and "the people who live in the nations." (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_metonymy]])
  * **the nations with fairness** - "he will judge the nations with fairness" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_ellipsis]])
  * **with fairness** - "honestly" or "using the same standard for everyone"
