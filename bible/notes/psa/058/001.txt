===== Translation Notes: =====

This psalm is a song about wicked people.

  * **For the chief musician**  - See how you translated this in [[en:bible:notes:psa:004:001|4:1]].
  * **set to Al Tashheth **  - See how you translated this in [[en:bible:notes:psa:057:001|57:1]].
  * **A Psalm of David**  - This can also be written as a sentence. AT: "This is a psalm of David" or "This is a psalm that David wrote"
  * **michtam **  - See how you translated this in [[:en:bible:notes:psa:016:001|16:1]].
  * **Do you indeed in silence speak righteousness **  - The psalmist, David, is using this question to rebuke the mighty men who are judges. AT: " When you mighty men speak, you never say what is right." (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_rquestion]])
  * **you, your** - The words "you" and "your" refer to the mighty men who are judges. The "you" here is plural (See: [[en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_youdual]]) 
  * **Do you judge uprightly, you sons of men **  - The psalmist, David, is using this question to emphasize that the mighty men judge unjustly. AT: "you never justly decide things about people." (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_rquestion]])
  * **you work wickedness in your heart **- AT: "Your plans are to do what is wrong." (See [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]])
  * **you weigh out the violence of your hands to the earth. **- The psalmist says the judges are not choosing justice but are working violence instead. AT: "You choose violence over justice." (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]])
