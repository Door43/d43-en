===== translationNotes: =====

  * **see** - The word "see" here alerts us to pay attention to the surprising information that follows.
  * **assembled themselves** - Here the kings represent their armies. AT: "assembled their armies" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_metonymy]])
  * **they passed by together** - "together they passed by Jerusalem"
  * **They saw it** - "They saw Jerusalem"
  * **dismayed** - "greatly troubled"
  * **Trembling took hold of them there** - The writer speaks of the kings' fear as if it were a person who caused the kings and their armies to tremble. AT: "There they trembled with fear" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_personification]])
  * **pain as when a woman is in labor** - The writer speaks of the kings' fear as if it were the pain that a woman experiences in childbirth and speaks of that pain as if it were a person. The verb may be supplied from the previous clause. AT: "pain took hold of them, as when a woman is in labor" or "they became afraid, like a woman is afraid of experiencing labor pains" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]] and [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_personification]] and [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_ellipsis]])
