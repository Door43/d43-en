===== translationNotes: =====

  * **You have withdrawn all your wrath** - Here God no longer punishing the Israelites is spoken of as if wrath were an object that Yahweh could take away from the Israelites. (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]])
  * **you have turned back from your hot anger** - Here God no longer being angry with the Israelites is spoken of as if anger were a fire that God from which God turned away. (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]])
  * **God of our salvation** - The abstract noun "salvation" can be stated as a verb. AT: "God who saves us" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_abstractnouns]])
  * **let go of your displeasure with us** - Asking God to stop being angry with the Israelites is spoken of as if displeasure were an object the writer wants God to let go of. (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]])
  * **Will you be angry with us forever? Will you remain angry throughout future generations?** - The writer uses these questions to emphasize that he is requesting God to stop being angry with them. These rhetorical questions can be translated as a statement. AT: "Please do not stay angry at us forever." (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_rquestion]])
