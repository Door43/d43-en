===== translationNotes: =====

  * **General Information:** - Parallelism is common in Hebrew poetry. (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:writing_poetry]] and [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_parallelism]])

  * **A psalm of David** - Possible meanings are 1) David wrote the psalm or 2) the psalm is about David or 3) the psalm is in the style of David's psalms.
  * **I have walked** - The word "walked" is a metaphor for behavior. AT: "I have behaved" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]])
  * **in Yahweh** - The third person use of "Yahweh" can be stated in the second person. AT: "in you" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_123person]])
  * **without wavering** - Doubting is spoken of as if it were losing balance and waving back and forth. AT: "without doubting" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]])
  * **Examine me** - "Try me" 
  * **test the purity of my inner parts and my heart** - Here "inner parts" and "heart" mean motives. AT: "test whether my motives are good" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_doublet]])
  * **For your covenant faithfulness is before my eyes** - Here "eyes" represent a person's thoughts and having something before one's eyes represents being aware of that thing. The abstract noun "faithfulness" can be translated as an adjective. AT: "For I am always aware of your covenant faithfulness" or "For I am always aware that you are faithful to your covenant" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]] and [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_abstractnouns]])
  * **I walk about in your faithfulness** - The word "walk" is a metaphor for behavior. The abstract noun "faithfulness" can be translated with an adjective. AT: "I conduct my life according to your faithfulness" or "I behave the way I do because you are faithful" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]] and [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_abstractnouns]])
