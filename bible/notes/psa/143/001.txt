===== translationNotes: =====

  * **General Information:** - Parallelism is common in Hebrew poetry. (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:writing_poetry]] and [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_parallelism]])

  * **A psalm of David** - Possible meanings are 1) David wrote the psalm or 2) the psalm is about David or 3) the psalm is in the style of David's psalms.
  * **Hear my prayer** - The words "my prayer" are a metonym for the person who is praying. See how these words are translated in [[:en:bible:notes:psa:039:012|39:12]]. AT: "Listen to me as I pray to you" or "Be willing to do what I ask you to do" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_metonymy]])
  * **answer me** - "please do what I ask you to do" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_idiom]])
  * **Do not enter into judgment** - To "enter into judgment" is an idiom that means to pronounce judgment. AT: "Please do not judge" or "I beg you not to judge" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_idiom]])
  * **your servant** - The psalmist speaks of himself as if he were speaking of another person. AT: "me" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_123person]])
  * **in your sight no one is righteous** - "you do not think anyone is righteous" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_synecdoche]])
