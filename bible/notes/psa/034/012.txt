===== translationNotes: =====

  * **What man is there who desires life and loves many days, that he may see good?** - The implicit answer to this question is "every man." This rhetorical question can be translated as a statement. AT: "Every man desires life and desires to live many days and have a good life" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_rquestion]])
  * **keep your tongue from evil ... keep your lips from speaking lies** - These two phrases refer to the same thing and it is said in different ways to emphasize its importance. (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_parallelism]])
  * **Then keep your tongue from evil** - Here "tongue" refers to the whole person. AT: "Therefore, do not speak evil" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_synecdoche]])
  * **keep your lips from speaking lies** - Here "lips" refers to the person speaking. AT: "do not speak lies" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_synecdoche]])
  * **Turn away from evil** - Here "Turn away" is a metaphor for avoiding evil. AT: "Refuse to do evil" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]])
  * **Seek peace** - Here "seek" means to be concerned about peace. AT: "Try hard to live in peace with other people" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]])
