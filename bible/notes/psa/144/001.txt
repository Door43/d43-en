===== translationNotes: =====

  * **General Information:** - Parallelism is common in Hebrew poetry. (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:writing_poetry]] and [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_parallelism]])

  * **A psalm of David** - Possible meanings are 1) David wrote the psalm or 2) the psalm is about David or 3) the psalm is in the style of David's psalms.
  * **my rock** - Possible meanings are 1) "the one who keeps me safe" or 2) "the one who gives me strength" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]])
  * **who trains my hands for war and my fingers for battle** - The words "hands" and "fingers" are synecdoches for "me." If "war" and "battle" are the same word in your language, you can translate this as one line. AT: "who trains me for war and trains me for battle" or "who trains me for war" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_synecdoche]] and [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_doublet]] and [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_ellipsis]])
  * **my covenant faithfulness** - The abstract noun "faithfulness" can be translated with anadverb. Possible meanings are 1) "the one who faithfully loves me" or 2) "the one who faithfully protects me." (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_abstractnouns]])
  * **my fortress ... take refuge** - The psalmist uses many metaphors to emphasize that Yahweh will protect him. (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]])
  * **my high tower** - David speaks of Yahweh as if he were a fortress that protects him from attack. Yahweh is the one who protects David from harm. (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]])
  * **my shield** - David speaks of Yahweh as if he were a shield that protects a soldier. Yahweh is the one who protects David from harm. See how this is translated in [[:en:bible:notes:psa:018:002|18:2]]. (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]])
  * **the one in whom I take refuge** - Going to Yahweh for protection is spoken of as taking refuge in him. AT: "the one to whom I go so he will protect me" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]])
  * **the one who subdues nations under me** - "the one who enables me to defeat other nations"
