===== translationNotes: =====

  * **General Information:** - Parallelism is common in Hebrew poetry. (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:writing_poetry]] and [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_parallelism]])

  * **A psalm of Asaph** - "This is a psalm that Asaph wrote." See how this is translated in [[:en:bible:notes:psa:053:001|53:1]].
  * **my feet almost slipped; my feet almost slipped out from under me** - The psalmist speaks of being unable to trust God and wanting to sin as if he had almost fallen while walking on a slippery path. AT: "I almost stopped trusting in God; I was almost guilty of committing a great sin against him" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]])
  * **I was envious of the arrogant** - "I envied the arrogant" or "I did not want arrogant people to have the good things that they had"
  * **the arrogant** - The adjective "arrogant" can be translated as a noun phrase. AT: "arrogant people" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_nominaladj]])
  * **the prosperity of the wicked** - The word "prosperity" can be translated as a verb. AT: "how the wicked have so many good things" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_abstractnouns]])
  * **the wicked** - The adjective "wicked" can be translated as a noun phrase. AT: "wicked people" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_nominaladj]])
