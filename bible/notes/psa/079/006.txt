===== translationNotes: =====

  * **General Information:** - These two pairs of phrases each share similar meanings and are combined for emphasis. (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_parallelism]])

  * **Pour out your wrath on the nations** - Asaph speaks of God's wrath as if it were a liquid. AT: "Since you are angry, punish the nations" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]])
  * **do not call upon your name** - The word "name" is a metonym for the person's power and authority. AT: "do not belong to you" or "do not ask you to help them" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_metonymy]])
  * **they have devoured Jacob** - The word "Jacob" is a mentonym for his descendants, the people of Israel. AT: "they have completely destroyed the people of Israel" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_metonymy]])
