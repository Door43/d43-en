===== translationNotes: =====

  * **Lord, where are your former acts of covenant faithfulness that you swore to David in your truthfulness?** - The writer uses a question to request the Lord to be faithful to his covenant with David. AT: "Lord, be faithful to your covenant with David as you have been in the past" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_rquestion]])
  * **your former acts of covenant faithfulness** - The abstract nouns "acts" and "faithfulness" can be translated with a verb and an adverb. AT: "the things that you did before that showed that you are faithful to your covenant" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_abstractnouns]])
  * **Call to mind** - The writer asks Yahweh to bring this up in his memory. AT: "Remember" or "Consider" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_idiom]])
  * **the mocking directed against your servants** - Here "your servants" refers to the people of Israel. AT: "how they mock us, your servants"
  * **how I bear in my heart so many insults from the nations** - Here "heart" represents the person's emotions. AT: "I endure so many insults from the people of the nations" or "I suffer because the people from the nations insult me" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_metonymy]])
  * **from the nations** - Here "nations" represents the people of the nations. (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_metonymy]])
  * **Your enemies hurl insults** - The enemies shouting insults at the king is spoken of as if insults were an object that the enemies throw violently at the king. (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]])
  * **they mock the footsteps of your anointed one** - Here "footsteps" represents where the king goes. AT: "they mock your anointed one wherever he goes" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_metonymy]])
