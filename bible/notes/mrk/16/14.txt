===== translationNotes: =====

  * **Connecting Statement:** - When Jesus meets with the eleven, he rebukes them for their unbelief and tells them to go out into all the world to preach the gospel.

  * **the eleven** - These are the eleven disciples who remained after Judas left them.
  * **they were reclining at the table** - This is a metonym for eating, which was the usual way people in that day ate meals. AT: "they were eating a meal" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_metonymy]])
  * **reclining** - They were lying down on the left side, using their right hand to eat the food on the table.
  * **hardness of heart** - Jesus is rebuking his disciples because they would not believe in him. Translate this idiom so it is understood that the disciples were not believing Jesus. AT: "refusal to believe" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_idiom]])
  * **Go into all the world** - Here "the world" is a metonym for the people in the world. AT: "Go everywhere there are people" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_metonymy]])
  * **the entire creation** - This is an exaggeration and a metonym for people everywhere. AT: "absolutely everybody" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_metonymy]] and [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_hyperbole]])
  * **He who believes and is baptized will be saved** - The word "He" refers to anyone. This sentence can be made active. AT: "God will save all people who believe and allow you to baptize them" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_activepassive]])
  * **he who does not believe will be condemned** - The word "he" refers to anyone. This clause can be made active. AT: "God will condemn all people who do not believe" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_activepassive]])
