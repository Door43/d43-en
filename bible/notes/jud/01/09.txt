===== Translation Notes: =====

  * **opposed** - This term means "disagreed with or disapproved of (something or someone)"
  * **...argued about the body...** - The UDB includes the term "possession", which adds the cause of the argument.
  * **dare** - the UDB does not use this term which adds a sense of fear of the devil.  I like the UDB approach.
  * **insulting** - This term means "being rude or offensive or showing a lack of respect"
  * **instead** - This term means "rather than an alternative choice" or "one thing is done or one thing or person is chosen when another is not chosen." 
  * **rebuke** - This term means "to speak in an angry and critical way to (someone)" 
  * **these people** - This phrase refers to the ungodly people initially mentioned in 1:04.
  * **understand** - This term means "to know the meaning of (something, such as the words that someone is saying or a language) 
  * **naturally** -  This term means "used to say that something is expected or normal" 
  * **like the creatures without a mind** - "Just like animals without a mind," (See Metaphore/Simile)  
  * **all the things that they understand naturally, like the creatures without a mind, in these things are they destroyed.** - Rephrased as: "regarding the evil things they do understand, they are like a mindless animal, in that they cannot recognize the destructive consequences of these bad things." (see Discussion below)   
  * **Cain** - This is the son of Adam who killed his brother (Able).
  * **they have walked in the way of Cain** - This phrase means they (ungodly men) have purposefully followed the behavior/manner of Cain. (See Metaphor)
   * **Balaam** - This is the evil god worshiped by these ungodly men. 
   * **wages** - This term means "money earned"   
   * **and have plunged into in the error of Balaam for wages** - This phrase can be rewritten as "They commit the same sin as the sin Balaam committed for money" - (UDB)
   * **Korah** - This name refers to an Israelite who rebelled against Moses.
   * **they have perished in Korah's rebellion** - This phrase can be rephrased as "They have died like Korah did when he rebelled against Moses." (Similar to UDB - for rephrased piece, see Metaphore/Simile)
