===== Translation Notes: =====

  * **bold words**  - explanation
  * **Let there be light**  - This is a command. God commanded that the light should exist.
  * **God saw the light, that it was good** - This can be translated as "God considered the light and was pleased with it." "Good" here means "pleasing" or "suitable."
  * **divided the light from the darkness** - This refers to God creating the daytime and the night time. It can be translated as "separated the light and the darkness" or "made it light at one time and dark at the another time."
  * **then there was**  - Creation is being told as a story. The use of "then" signifies the next part. After God separated light from darkness, then there was evening and morning. It describes the event that followed the separation.
  * **evening and morning**  - This is way of referring to a day. A day includes both nighttime and daytime. In the Jewish culture, a day begins when the sun sets.
  * **one day**  - This refers to that first day when God separated from the light from the darkness. It can be translated as "the first day."
