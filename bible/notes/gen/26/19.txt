====== Genesis 26:19-20 ======


===== TFT: =====

<usfm>
\v 19 Isaac's servants dug in the valley and discovered a well of fresh water.
\v 20 But the men who lived in Gerar Valley who took care of their animals argued\f + \ft Or: quarreled \f* with the men who took care of Isaac's animals, and said, "The water in this well is ours!" So Isaac named the well Esek, which means 'dispute,' because they disputed about who owned it.
</usfm>


===== UDB: =====

\v 19 When Isaac’s servants dug in the valley, they found there a well of flowing water.

===== Translation Notes: =====

  * **flowing water**  - This phrase refers to water that is safe to drink. This could also be translated as "fresh water" (UDB).
  * **Esek**  - "Translators may also add a footnote that says “The name Esek means 'quarrel' or 'argue' or 'dispute.'"
