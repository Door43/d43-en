===== translationNotes: =====

  * **General Information:** - These verses are almost identical to [[:en:bible:notes:jer:49:20|49:20]] and [[:en:bible:notes:jer:49:21|49:21]]. See how you translated those verses. 

  * **the plans that Yahweh has decided against Babylon, the plans that he has planned against the land of the Chaldeans** - These two phrases have the same meaning and are used together for emphasis. AT: "the plans that Yahweh has decided for the people of Babylon and the Chaldeans" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_parallelism]])
  * **They will certainly be dragged away, even the smallest flock** - This continues speaking of Yahweh punishing the people of Babylon as if he would come like a lion and attack the sheep. This can be stated in active form. AT: "He will drag them away, even the smallest flock" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]] and [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_activepassive]])
  * **even the smallest flock** - Here the youngest and weakest people of Babylon are spoken of as if they were the smallest flock of sheep. AT: "even the youngest and weakest" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]])
  * **Their pasturelands will be turned into ruined places** - Here the land of Babylonia is spoken of as if it were pasturelands for flocks. This can be stated in active form. AT: "He will turn their pasturelands into ruined places" or "He will completely destroy the people who live there" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]] and [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_activepassive]])
  * **At the sound of conquered Babylon the earth shakes** - This phrase compares the fall of the once powerful nation of Babylon to the extremely loud noise of an earthquake. AT: "The fall of Babylon will sound like a mighty earthquake" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]])
  * **distress** - pain or unhappiness
  * **their shout of distress** - "their shout of pain"
