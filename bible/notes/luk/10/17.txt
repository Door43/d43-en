===== Translation Notes: =====


    * **And the seventy returned**  - These are the seventy people that Jesus had sent to the villages. Some languages will need to say that the seventy actually went out first as the UDB does. This is implicit information that can be made explicit. (See [[en:ta:vocabulary:implicitexplicit|Implicit Information]].)
    * **I was watching Satan fall from heaven as lightening** - Jesus used a simile to describe how God was defeating Satan when his 70 disciples were preaching in the towns. 
    * **Behold, I have given you authority** - The word "Behold" alerts us to the task that Jesus is giving new authority to the disciples in the story. Your language may have a way of doing this. English uses "And now I am giving you the authority...‚Äù
    * **authority to tread on serpents and scorpions**  - "authority to step on and crush snakes and scorpions." Crushing snakes and scorpions is a metaphor for defeating evil spirits. In the UDB it is translated without the metaphor: "I have given you the right to attack evil spirits." (See: [[en:ta:figuresofspeech:metaphor]])
    * **over all the power of the enemy**  - "I have given you authority to crush the power of the enemy" or "I have given you authority to defeat the enemy." The enemy is Satan.
    * **in this** - The phrase "that the spirits submit to" explains what "this" is.
    * **your names are engraved in heaven**  - This can be translated as an active verb "God has written your names in heaven" or "your names are on the list of people who are citizens of heaven."
