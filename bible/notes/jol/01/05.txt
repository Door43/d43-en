===== Translation Notes: =====

  * **you drunkards** This refers to the nation of Israel. Alternate translation: "senseless or unconcerned people." (See:[[en:ta:translate:metonymy]])
  * **drunkards... drinkers of wine** -  "drunkards" and "drinkers of wine" mean basically the same thing. It used her for emphasis of how far Israel had fallen away. Alternate translation: "you drunken, careless nation" (See:[[en:ta:translate:doublet]])
  * **nation** - This "nation" is referring to the locust swarm.(See:[[en:ta:translate:personification]])
  * **my land** - This is refers to the nation of Israel. Alternate translation: "the nation of Israel" or "the land of Yahweh"(See:[[en:ta:translate:metonymy]])
  * **Strong and without number** - This refers back to "nation." It shows that the "nation" is both "strong" and "without number." This says basically the same thing. Here it emphasizes the power of the "nation." Alternate translation: "the mighty army is uncountable in number." (See:[[en:ta:translate:doublet]])
  * **His teeth are the teeth of a lion,
And he has the teeth of a lioness** - The locusts mandibles or mouth parts have the look of a lions teeth. They are however not actually lion or lioness' teeth. Additionally, lions don't just kill they typically tear open their prey. Alternate translation: "The locust mouth look like a lion or lioness' mouth." or "The locust will destroy you like a lion or lioness would." (See:[[en:ta:translate:metaphor]])
  * **His... he** - These refers to the invading nation.
  * **He has made my vineyard into a terrifying place**  - This phrase is a metaphor. The vineyard represents the land, and the land is terrifying due to the nation invading the land mentioned in 1:6. (See:[[en:ta:translate:metaphor]])
  * **made my vineyard into a terrifying place... stripped my fig tree bare... stripped its bark and thrown it away... branches are bare white."** - This emphasizes the completeness of the destruction of Israel. It is made completely barren the vines and the trees killed. Alternate translation: "all you ability to make wine or produce food is gone."  (See:[[en:ta:translate:parallelism]])
