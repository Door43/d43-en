===== Translation Notes: =====

The author writes about the 2 pillars, named Jakin and Boaz.

  * **capital**  - In this verse, capital refers to a decoration on top of the column or pillar.
  * **two hundred**  - or 200. Decide how you want to translate numbers. (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:translate_numbers|Translate Numbers]])
  * **pomegranate**  - see 7:18
  * **He raised up the pillars**  - "He" refers to Huram and his workers. (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_inclusive|Inclusive]])
  * **He raised up the pillars at the temple portico.**  - The workers lifted the pillars and stood them up. It is implicit that the pillars were cast or made on the ground and then Huram and his workers lifted the pillars and stood them up in front of the temple portico. (See [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_explicit|Explicit and Implicit]])
  * **portico**  - see 6:3
  * **Jakin**  - The name Jakin, also spelled Jachin, means God establishes.
  * **Boaz**  - Boaz was the great-great-grandfather of Solomon.
  * **On the top of the pillars were decorations like lilies.**  - AT: "These tops were placed on a bowl shaped section" (UDB).
  * **lily/lilies**  - see 7:19
