===== Translation Notes: =====

The lover continues to praise the woman.
  * **my bride is a garden locked up** - Solomon is using a word picture to say that no one else may have his wife. She is saved sexually for him alone.  (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_euphemism]] and [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]])
  * **Your branches are a grove of pomegranate trees with choice fruit, and of henna . . . with all the finest spices** - These are pictures he is using to show how desirable his bride is to him. (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]]) 
  * **henna, nard, spikenard, saffron, calamus, cinnamon, myrrh, aloes**  - These are parts of plants used for perfumes, food flavoring, and medicines.
  * **henna** - See how you translated this in [[en:bible:notes:sng:01:12|1:14]].
  * **nard** - See how you translated this in [[en:bible:notes:sng:01:12|1:12]].
  * **spikenard** - See how you translated this in [[en:bible:notes:sng:04:12|4:14]]
  * **saffron** - "Saffron" is a spice that comes from the dried parts from the yellow thread in the center of a certain flower. 
  * **calamus** - a root used to aid people with stomach problems
  * **cinnamon** - a sweet cooking spice made from the bark of a tree
  * **myrrh** - See how you translated this in [[en:bible:notes:sng:01:12|1:13]].
  * **aloes** - tropical plants with thick leaves which have a thick liquid used in medicine or beauty creams
