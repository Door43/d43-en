===== translationNotes: =====

  * **I am dark but lovely** - "My skin is dark, but I am still beautiful" or "Even though my skin is dark, I am beautiful"
  * **daughters of Jerusalem** - "young women of Jerusalem." These young women could not hear her and were not present, but the woman speaks as if they were present and could hear her. (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_apostrophe]])
  * **dark like the tents of Kedar** - The nomadic tribes in Kedar used black goat skins to build their homes. The woman is comparing her skin to these tents. (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_simile]])
  * **lovely like the curtains of Solomon** - Solomon produced beautiful curtains either for his own palace or for the Temple. She says that her skin is beautiful. (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_simile]])
  * **scorched** - This exaggeration for "burned" or "made black" refers to the sun changing her skin from light to dark. (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_hyperbole]])
  * **My mother's sons** - "My half-brothers." These brothers probably had the same mother as the woman but not the same father.
  * **made me keeper of the vineyards** - "made me take care of the vineyards"
  * **but my own vineyard I have not kept** - The woman compares herself to a vineyard. AT: "but I have not been able to take care of myself" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]])
