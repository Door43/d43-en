===== translationNotes: =====

  * **Ben Hadad** - This is the name of the king of Aram. His name means "son of Hadad." See how you translated this man's name in [[:en:bible:notes:2ki:06:24|6:24]]. (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:translate_names]])
  * **Hazael** - This is the name of a man. (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:translate_names]])
  * **Take a gift** - Hazael was to take many gifts, not just one. AT: "take many gifts" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_synecdoche]])
  * **in your hand** - The phrase "in your hand" is an idiom meaning for him to take the gifts with him. AT: "with you" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_idiom]])
  * **the man of God** - "Elisha, the man of God"
  * **consult with Yahweh through him, saying** - "ask Elisha to ask Yahweh"
  * **carried by forty camels** - This can be stated in active form. AT: "which forty camels carried" 
  * **forty camels** - "40 camels" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:translate_numbers]])
  * **Your son Ben Hadad king of Aram** - Ben Hadad was not actually Elisha's son, but Hazael called him that to show a close relationship between them. AT: "Ben Hadad, the king of Aram, who is like a son to you," (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]])
