===== translationNotes: =====

  * **an aroma from death to death** - The word "aroma" refers to knowledge about Christ. For those who are spiritually dead, knowledge about Christ is like the smell of a dead, decaying body. AT: "knowledge about death to those who are dead." (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]])
  * **aroma** - "fragrance" or "good smell"
  * **aroma from life to life** - The word "aroma" refers to knowledge about Christ. For those who are spiritually alive, knowledge about Christ is like a sweet-smelling fragrance. AT: "knowledge about life to those who are alive." (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]])
  * **Who is worthy of these things?** - Paul uses this question to express that the knowledge of Christ is a gift from God, which no one deserves. AT: "No one is worthy of these things." (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_rquestion]])
  * **purity of motives** - "honest desires"
  * **we speak in Christ** - "we speak because of our belief in Christ"
