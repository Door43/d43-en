===== Translation Notes: =====

Jesus begins to tell a parable about faithful and unfaithful servants.

  * **it is like** - The word "it" here refers to "the kingdom of heaven." (See 25:1) 
  * **was about to go** - "was ready to go" or "was to go soon"
  * **gave over to them his wealth** - "put them in charge of his wealth"
  * **his wealth** - "his property"
  * **five talents** - A "talent" was worth twenty year's wages. Translators should avoid putting this into modern currency. The important point for the parable is to communicate the relative amounts of five, two and one as well as the large amounts of wealth involved. (UDB: "five bags of gold") (See: [[en:obe:other:biblicalmoney]])
  * **he went on his journey** - The words "he" and "his" refer to the man or master who was about to go into another country.
  * **and made another five talents** - "and earned another five talents"
