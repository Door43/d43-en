===== translationNotes: =====

  * **General Information:** - David sings a song of mourning for Saul and Jonathan.

  * **Song of the Bow** - This was the title of the song.
  * **which has been written in The Book of Jashar** - This is background information added to tell the reader what happened to the song in the future. (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:writing_background]])
  * **The Book of Jashar** - The word "Jashar" means "upright." AT: "The Book of the Upright" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:translate_names]])
  * **Your glory, Israel, is dead** - "Your glory" refers to Saul. (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_metonymy]])
  * **the mighty** - The phrase "the mighty" refers to both Saul and Jonathan. This nominal adjective is plural, and can be stated as "the mighty ones."  (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_metonymy]] and [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_nominaladj]]) 
  * **have fallen** - The word "fallen" here means "died." (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_euphemism]])
  * **Do not tell it in Gath ... do not proclaim it in the streets of Ashkelon** - These two phrases mean the same thing and are repeated as part of the poetry of the song. (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_parallelism]])
  * **Gath ... Ashkelon** - Gath and Ashkelon are two of the Philistines' major cities. The Philistines killed Saul and Jonathan. 
  * **so that the daughters of the Philistines may not rejoice ... so that the daughters of the uncircumcised may not celebrate** - These two phrases mean the same thing and are repeated as part of the poetry of the song. (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_parallelism]])
  * **the daughters of the uncircumcised** - This phrase refers to people who do not follow Yahweh, such as the Philistines. (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_metonymy]])
