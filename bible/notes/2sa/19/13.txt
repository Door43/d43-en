===== translationNotes: =====

  * **Amasa** - Translate this man's name the same as you did in [[:en:bible:notes:2sa:17:24|17:25]]. (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:translate_names]])
  * **Are you not my flesh and my bone?** - David uses this rhetorical question to emphasize that they are related. He speaks of them being related by saying that they have the same flesh and bones. AT: "You are my flesh and my bone." (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_rquestion]])
  * **my flesh and my bone** - Here David speaks of them being related by saying that they have the same flesh and bones. Also, see how you translated a similar phrase in [[:en:bible:notes:2sa:19:11|19:12]]. AT: "my relative" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]])
  * **he won the hearts** - Hear "hearts" means affection or loyalty. AT: "he won the loyalty" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_synecdoche]])
  * **the Jordan** - the Jordan River
