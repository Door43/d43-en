===== translationNotes: =====


  * **If it pleases the king** - "If what I ask for pleases the king"
  * **if I have found favor in your sight** - "Find favor" is an idiom that means be approved of or that he is pleased with her. "In your sight" is a metaphor representing his evaluation. AT: "if you evaluate me and approve" or "if you are pleased with me" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_idiom]] and [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]])
  * **if the thing seems right before the king** - "Before the king" is a metaphor representing his evaluation. AT: "if what I ask for seems right in the king's evaluation" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]])
  * **and I am pleasing in your eyes** - "In your sight" is a metaphor representing his evaluation. AT: "and if you evaluate me and I please you" or "if you are pleased with me" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]])
  * **revoke** - "officially cancel"
  * **Hammedatha** - This is the name of a man. (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:translate_names]])
  * **provinces** - A "province" is a large area into which some countries are divided for the purposes of government. See how you translated this in [[:en:bible:notes:est:01:01|1:1]].
  * **For how could I bear to see disaster fall on my people? How could I endure watching the destruction of my relatives?** - Esther used these questions to show that that she would be extremely sad if her people were to be destroyed. AT: "I cannot bear to see disaster fall on the Jews. I cannot endure watching my relatives be killed." (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_rquestion]])
