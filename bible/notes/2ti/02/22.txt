===== translationNotes: =====

  * **Flee youthful lusts** - Here "Flee" is an idiom that means to avoid. AT: "Completely avoid youthful lusts" or "Do not desire the things that young people usually desire" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_idiom]])
  * **Pursue righteousness** - Here "Pursue" means the opposite of "Flee." It is an idiom that means to try hard to obtain something. AT: "Try your best to obtain righteousness" or "Seek after righteousness" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_idiom]])
  * **with those** - Possible meanings are 1) Paul wants Timothy to join with other believers in pursuing righteousness, faith, love, and peace, or 2) Paul wants Timothy to be a peace and not argue with other believers.
  * **those who call on the Lord** - Here "call on the Lord" is an idiom that means to trust and worship the Lord. AT: "those who worship the Lord" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_idiom]])
  * **out of a clean heart** - Here "heart" refers to a person's desires or motives. And, "clean" means honest or sincere. AT: "with honest motives" or "sincerely" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_metonymy]])
  * **refuse foolish and ignorant questions** - "refuse to answer foolish and ignorant questions." Paul means that the people who ask such questions are foolish. AT: "refuse to answer the question that foolish and ignorant people ask" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_metonymy]])
  * **they give birth to arguments** - Paul speaks of ignorant questions as if they were women giving birth to children. AT: "they cause arguments" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]])
