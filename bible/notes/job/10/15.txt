===== translationNotes: =====

  * **If I am wicked** - "If I am guilty" or "If I do evil things"
  * **woe to me** - "how terrible will it be for me"
  * **even if I were righteous** - "even if I always did things rightly"
  * **lift up my head** - This idiom means to be sure or confident. AT: "hold my head up" or "be confident" or "be sure about myself" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_idiom]])
  * **I am filled with disgrace** - The abstract noun "disgrace" can be translated using the adjective "disgraced." AT: "I am filled up with being disgraced" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_abstractnouns]])
  * **disgrace** - "shame"
  * **am looking at my own suffering** - The abstract noun "suffering" can be translated using the verb "suffer." AT: "am looking at how I suffer" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_abstractnouns]])
  * **and am looking** - The missing word can be supplied. AT: "and I am looking" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_ellipsis]])
  * **If my head lifts itself** - This idiom means to become self-confident or proud. AT: "If I become proud" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_idiom]] and [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_rpronouns]])
  * **you hunt me down like a lion** - Possible meanings of this simile are 1) God hunts Job like a lion hunts its prey or 2) Job is like a lion being hunted by God. (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_simile]])
  * **once again you show yourself powerful to me** - This phrase expresses irony in how God's marvelous power is displayed in how he goes against Job. (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_irony]] and [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_rpronouns]])
