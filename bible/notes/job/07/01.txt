===== translationNotes: =====

  * **General Information:** - The writer continues to use parallelism in each of these verses, conveying a single idea using two different statements to emphasize that his personal suffering is part of the universal suffering which all people experience. (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_parallelism]])

  * **Does not man have hard labor on earth?** - Job poses this negative question to emphasize his awareness that all people experience hard work. It can be translated as a positive statement. AT: "There is hard labor for every person on earth." (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_rquestion]])
  * **on earth** - This is a generalization that means for the time people are living on the earth. AT: "while he lives on the earth" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_hyperbole]])
  * **Are not his days like the days of a hired man?** - Job poses this negative question to emphasize his awareness that all people struggle in life. AT: "And their days are like the days of a hired man." (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_rquestion]] and [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_simile]])
  * **Like a slave ... like a hired man ... so I have been ... given trouble-filled nights** - Job compares his misery and trouble to that of the slave and hired man. (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_simile]])
  * **the shadows of evening** - "cool shade." The implied information is that the shadows of evening provide coolness and shade from the sun (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_explicit]])
  * **a hired man** - "a day laborer." This is a man who worked jobs one day at a time and was paid at the end of every day.
  * **looks for his wages** - "waits for his pay" 
  * **so I have been made to endure months of misery; I have been given trouble-filled nights** - This can be translated in active form. AT: "so I endure months of misery; I get trouble-filled nights" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_activepassive]])
  * **months of misery** - The abstract noun "misery" can be translated as the adjective "miserable." AT: "miserable months" or "empty months" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_explicit]])
