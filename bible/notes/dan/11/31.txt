===== Translation Notes: =====

  * **His forces will rise up** - AT: "His army will prepare for battle" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_idiom]])
  * **profane the sanctuary** - this phrase was used when the place where God was worshiped was treated by people in ways that made it "unclean" or unacceptable for the worship of God, and offensive to God himself.
  * **the abomination which causes destruction** - vile, evil and defiling actions that God will destroy
  * **flatter ... seduce** - flatter, as in enticement to do something wrong, or seduce, as in commit infidelity
  * **who know their God** - a common way, especially in the NT, of describing those who believe in and trust in God; this is the only place in the OT where this phrase occurs
