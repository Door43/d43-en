===== translationNotes: =====

  * **Judah and Jerusalem** - "those living in Judah and Jerusalem" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_metonymy]])
  * **In the latter days** - "In the future"
  * **the mountain of Yahweh's house will be established** - This can be stated 1) as a description AT: "The mountain of Yahweh's house will stand" or 2) as an active form. AT: "Yahweh will establish the mountain on which his temple is built" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_activepassive]])
  * **as the highest of the mountains** - Isaiah speaks of importance as if it were physical height. AT: "the most important of the mountains" or "the most important place in the world" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]])
  * **and will be exalted above the hills** - Isaiah speaks of honor in metaphor as if it were physical height. (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]]) This can be stated 1) AT: in active form, "and Yahweh will honor it more than any other hill" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_activepassive]]) or 2) as a metonym for the people who worship there AT: "and Yahweh will honor the people who worship there more than he honors any other people." (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_metonymy]])
  * **and all nations** - "and people from all nations" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_metonymy]])
  * **will flow to it** - The people around the world going to the mountain of Yahweh is compared to how a river flows. This emphasizes that many people will come, not just a few people. AT: "will flow like a river to it" or "will go to it" (UDB) (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]])
