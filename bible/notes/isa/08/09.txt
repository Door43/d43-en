===== Translation Notes: =====

  * **You peoples will be broken to pieces** - The phrase "you peoples" refers to the armies who try to attack Judah. Alternate translation: "I will break your armies to pieces" (See: [[en:ta:translate:active]])
  * **Listen, all you distant countries** - Isaiah stops talking to the people of Judah and begins to warn those who may try to attack Judah.  This can be translated as "Listen, all you people in far away places." (See: [[en:ta:translate:apostrophe]])
  * **arm yourselves for war and be broken in pieces; arm yourselves and be broken in pieces** - This is two ways of saying the same thing. It emphasizes that God will defeat the enemy armies. This can be translated as, "you can prepare yourselves for battle, but I will defeat you." (See: [[en:ta:translate:doublet]])
  * **Form a plan, but it will not be carried out; issue the command, but it will not be carried out** - These are two ways of saying the same thing. It emphasizes that the plans of the enemies will not work. Alternate translation: "You can prepare to attack Judah, but you will not succeed."
  * **God is with us** - This is the meaning of the name "Immanuel."
