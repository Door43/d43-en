===== translationNotes: =====

  * **General Information:** - Isaiah continues to speak about the people and the city of Samaria as if they were a garland. (See: [[:en:bible:notes:isa:28:01|28:1-2]])

  * **The proud garland of the drunkards of Ephraim will be trodden underfoot** - This can be stated in active form. AT: "The enemy army will crush the proud drunkards of Samaria as if they were crushing flowers under their feet" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_activepassive]])
  * **proud garland ... of Ephraim ... that is on the head of the rich valley** - Here "proud garland" is a metonym that represents the proud people who wear the garland. It is also a metaphor that speaks of the city of Samaria, which is locoated at the top of a fertile valley, as if it were a garland on the head of the valley. (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_metonymy]] and [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]])
  * **will be as the first ripe fig ... gulps it down** - This speaks of the enemy army seeing the beauty of Samaria and quickly plundering it as if it were a person who sees the first fig of the season and quickly eats it. (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_simile]])
