===== Translation Notes: =====

  * **Woe to the proud garland of the drunkards of Ephraim** - "Terrible things will happen to the proud drunks of the city of Ephraim"
  * **and to the fading flower of his glorious beauty** - The upcoming destruction of Ephraim, also known as Samaria, the Capital of Israel is compared to that of a beautiful flower that dies and wilts. (See: Metaphor)
  * **that is on the head of the lush valley of those who are overcome with wine** - "the city is on a hill above a fertile valley and the people who live there get drunk by too much wine"
  * **Behold** - "Listen"
  * **the Lord has a mighty and strong one; as a hailstorm...overwhelming** - In this Simile, the soldiers of the army of Yahweh are compared to a hailstorm or strong wind that will destroy everything in its path. (See: Simile)
  * **He will strike the earth with his hand**  - The "hand" represents the power of Yahweh. "Hand" is a Metonymy for "powerful destruction." This means that Yahweh will destroy Samaria with his powerful hand. Alternate Translation: "He will destroy the city of Samaria by his power" (See: Metonymy)
