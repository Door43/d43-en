===== translationNotes: =====

  * **General Information:** - Isaiah speaks to the people of Judah in the form of a poem. (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_parallelism]])

  * **Your country is ruined** - This can be stated in active form. AT: "They have ruined your country" or "Your enemies have ruined your country" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_activepassive]])
  * **your cities are burned** - This can be stated in active form. AT: "they have burned your cities" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_activepassive]])
  * **your fields—in your presence, strangers are destroying them** - "people who are not from your own country steal the crops from your fields while you watch"
  * **abandoned devastation** - "empty and devastated."  This abstract noun phrase can be expressed as verb phrases.  AT: "they have destroyed the land and no one lives there" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_abstractnouns]])
  * **overthrown by strangers** - This can be stated in active form. AT: "strangers have overthrown your country" or "a foreign army has completely conquered it" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_activepassive]])
  * **The daughter of Zion is left** - This can be stated in active form. AT: "I have left the daughter of Zion" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_activepassive]])
  * **The daughter of Zion** - The "daughter" of a city means the people of the city. AT: "The people of Zion" or "The people who live in Zion" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_idiom]])
  * **is left like a hut in a vineyard, like a shed in a garden of cucumbers** - Possible meanings are 1) "has become as small as a hut in a vineyard or a shed in a garden of cucumbers" or 2) "is left the way a farmer leaves a hut in a vineyard or a shed in a garden of cucumbers when he is finished with them" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_simile]] and [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_explicit]])
  * **like ... cucumbers, like a besieged city** - Another possible meaning is "like ... cucumbers. She is a besieged city"
