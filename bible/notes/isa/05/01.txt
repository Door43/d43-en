===== translationNotes: =====

  * **General Information:** - Isaiah tells a parable about a farmer and his vineyard. The farmer represents God and the vineyard represents the people of Judah, the southern kingdom of the Israelites. (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_parables]])

  * **my well beloved** - "my dear friend"
  * **on a very fertile hill** - "on a hill where very good crops could grow"
  * **He spaded it** - This expresson refers to using a tool to dig into the ground to prepare it for planting. AT: "He prepared the soil"
  * **He built a tower in the middle of it** - "He made a tall building in the middle of the vineyard to watch over it." Someone would stand at the top of the tower to watch the vineyard and make sure that no animals or people went into it.
  * **built a winepress** - A winepress is a low place carved out of rock in the ground where workers step on the grapes to crush them with their feet, in order to remove the grape juice. AT: "dug a pit to squeeze out the grape juice"
  * **wild grapes** - "worthless grapes" or "bad tasting grapes"
