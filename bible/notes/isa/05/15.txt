===== translationNotes: =====

  * **Man will be forced to bend down, and mankind will be humbled** - The phrases used together mean the same thing and are used together for emphasis. This can be stated in active form. See how you translated similar words in [[:en:bible:notes:isa:02:09|2:9]]. AT: "God will make every person bow down and be humble" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_parallelism]] and [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_activepassive]])
  * **Man will be forced to bend down, and mankind will be humbled** - Future events are spoken of as if they have already happened.   (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_pastforfuture]]) 
  * **Man will be forced to bend down** - Bending down often symbolizes being humiliated. (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:translate_symaction]])
  * **the eyes of the lofty will be cast down** - Looking down is often a sign of being ashamed. See how you translated similar words in [[:en:bible:notes:isa:02:09|2:11]]. AT: "the eyes of proud people will look down in shame" or "people who were proud are now ashamed" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_metonymy]])
  * **the lofty** - Here proud, arrogant people are spoken of as if they are high above other people. AT: "proud" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]]) 
  * **Yahweh of hosts will be exalted in his justice** - This can be stated in active form. AT: "People will praise Yahweh of hosts because he is just" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_activepassive]])
  * **Yahweh of hosts** - See how you translated this in [[:en:bible:notes:isa:01:09|1:9]].
  * **will be exalted** - Being honored is spoken of as if it were being lifted high. AT: "will be greatly honored" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]]) 
  * **the sheep will feed as in their own pasture** - Yahweh will destroy the city of Jerusalem, which was called a "vineyard" in [[:en:bible:notes:isa:05:01|5:1]]. It will become good for nothing except for sheep to eat grass there.
  * **graze** - eat grass
  * **in the ruins, lambs will graze as foreigners** - That is, the lambs will graze there. The land will be worthless for any other use. (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_simile]])
