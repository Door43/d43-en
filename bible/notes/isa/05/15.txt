===== translationNotes: =====

  * **Man is brought down, and the great man is humbled** - The phrases used together mean the same thing and are used together for emphasis. This can be stated in active form. AT: "God will make every person bow down and be humble" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_parallelism]] and [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_activepassive]]). See how you translated similar words in [[:en:bible:notes:isa:02:09|2:9]].
  * **the eyes of the lofty are cast down** - "the eyes of proud people are looking down" in shame. AT: "people who were proud are now ashamed" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_metonymy]]) See how you translated similar words in [[:en:bible:notes:isa:02:09|2:11]].
  * **Yahweh of hosts** - See how you translated this in [[:en:bible:notes:isa:01:09|1:9]].
  * **Yahweh of hosts is exalted in his justice** - This can be stated in active form. AT: "People will praise Yahweh of hosts because he is just" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_activepassive]])
  * **the sheep will feed as in their own pasture** - Yahweh will destroy the city of Jerusalem, the "vineyard" of [[:en:bible:notes:isa:05:01|5:1]], so thoroughly that it will become as good a place for sheep to graze as their own pasture.
  * **graze** - eat grass
  * **in the ruins of the rich people, lambs will graze** - Possible meanings are 1) "lambs will eat the grass that grows where rich people's houses once stood" or 2) this is a metaphor, "foreigners will be able to live well where the houses once stood."  (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]])
