===== translationNotes: =====

  * **He who winks the eye** - "Winking the eye" represents a secretive sign for being cruel to someone else. AT: "He who makes a signal with a gesture" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_metonymy]])
  * **will be thrown down** - A person who is ruined is spoken of as if he were thrown down. AT: "others will ruin him" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]] and [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_activepassive]])
  * **The mouth of the righteous** - "The words of a righteous person" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_metonymy]])
  * **is a water spring of life** - This person's speech is spoken of as if it preserved living animals or people, as a water spring would do in a dry land. (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]])
  * **the mouth of the wicked** - "Mouth" represents what a persons says. AT: "the words of a wicked person" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_metonymy]])
  * **the mouth of the wicked covers up violence** - That is, the wicked person appears to say harmless things, but plans to violent things against other people. (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]])
