===== translationNotes: =====

  * **The heart of a wise person** - Here the word "heart" represents the mind and thoughts. AT: "The thoughts of a wise person" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_metonymy]])
  * **gives insight to his mouth** - Here the word "mouth" is a metonym for speech. AT: "makes his speech wise" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_metonymy]])
  * **to his lips** - Here the word "mouth" is a metonym for speech. "to what he says" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_metonymy]])
  * **Pleasant words are a honeycomb** - The writer speaks of pleasant words being able to bring delight to the hearers as if the words were a honeycomb dripping with fresh honey. AT: "Pleasant words are like a honeycomb" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]])
  * **sweet to the soul** - Possible meanings for the word "soul" are 1) it represents a person's inward desires and pleasures. AT: "sweet enough to make a person happy" or 2) it can mean "throat" and is a metonym for a person's tongue and ability to taste. AT: "sweet to a person's taste" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_metonymy]])
  * **healing to the bones** - Here the word "bones" represent a person's body. AT: "healing to the body" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_metonymy]])
