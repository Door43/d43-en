===== translationNotes: =====

  * **Do not let covenant faithfulness and trustworthiness ever leave you** - The writer speaks of "covenant faithfulness" and "trustworthiness" as if they were people who could leave someone. The negative command can be stated positively. AT: "Make sure always to practice covenant faithfulness and to be trustworthy" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_personification]])
  * **tie them together about your neck** - The writer speaks of faithfulness and trustworthiness as if they were objects that a person could tie around the neck like a necklace. The image suggests that these are valuable things that the person displays outwardly. AT: "display them proudly like one would wear a necklace" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]])
  * **write them on the tablet of your heart** - Here the heart represents a person's mind and is spoken of as if it were a tablet upon which someone can write messages and commands. AT: "always remember them, as if you had written them permanently on a tablet" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_metonymy]] and [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]])
  * **in the sight of God and man** - The writer speaks of God's and man's opinions of something as if they were seeing that thing. AT: "in the opinion of God and man" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]])
