===== Translation Notes: =====

  * **I**  - These pronouns refer to Yahweh.
  * **you/yourselves/your/us**  - These pronouns refer to “the children” which is the house (or people) of Israel.
  * **And whore about after detestable things?**  - The word "whore" refers to an unfaithful woman. In this usage, God is comparing the people of Israel to an unfaithful wife who is untrue to her husband.
  * **detestable things**  - abominations, abhorrences, filthy or disgusting things
  * **For when you lift up your gifts**  - The phrase "lift up your gifts" refers to their gifts of sacrifice.
  * **and cause your sons to pass through the fire**  - The sacrifice (killing by burning) of children was a common practice in the worship of other gods.
  * **inquired**  - This word means “to ask.” In the Bible, it means to go to a prophet and ask what God says.
  * **As I live**  - God is swearing on His eternal nature.
  * **This is the Lord Yahweh's declaration**  - Synonyms for declaration include “announcement” or “proclamation”. An alternate translation could read “This is the Lord Yahweh's strong statement.”
  * **The thing going up into your spirit will not be real**  - An alternate translation could read, "The thing you are thinking will not come to pass." Compare to "But what you want will never happen." (UDB)
  * **to serve wood and stone**  - "Wood and stone" is a metonym for "idol." An alternate translation could read "to serve idols." "To worship idols made of wood and stone" (UDB) (See: Metonymy).
