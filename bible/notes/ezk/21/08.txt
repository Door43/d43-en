===== translationNotes: =====

  * **the word of Yahweh came** - This is an idiom that is used to introduce something that God told his prophets or his people. See how you translated this in [[:en:bible:notes:ezk:03:16|3:16]]. AT: "Yahweh spoke this message" or "Yahweh spoke these words" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_idiom]])
  * **A sword! A sword! It will be sharpened and polished!** - This passage describes the sword of Yahweh which is a metaphor for an enemy army that Yahweh will use to attack Jerusalem. This metaphor begins here and continues through [[:en:bible:notes:ezk:21:15|21:17]]. (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]])
  * **It will be sharpened and polished** - This phrase indicates that the sword is ready for someone to use it. This can be stated in active form. AT: "It is sharp and polished" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_activepassive]])
  * **polished** - Someone made the sword smooth, shiny, and clean by rubbing it with a rough material.
