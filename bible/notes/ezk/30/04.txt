===== translationNotes: =====

  * **General Information:** - These are Yahweh's words about Egypt.

  * **Then a sword will come against Egypt** - The word "sword" here represents war or an army that attacks. AT: "There will be war against Egypt" or "An enemy army will attack Egypt" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_metonymy]])
  * **there will be anguish in Cush** - The abstract noun "anguish" can be expressed in terms of how the people feel. AT: "the people of Cush will be very sad" or "the people of Cush will suffer" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_abstractnouns]])
  * **when the killed people fall in Egypt** - Possible meanings are that 1) "fall" is a metonym for dying. AT: "when people in Egypt are killed" or 2) "fall" refers to their bodies falling and lying on the ground. AT: "when people in Egypt are killed and lie fallen" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_metonymy]])
  * **when they take her wealth** - "when the attackers take away the wealth of Egypt"
  * **when her foundations are ruined** - This can be translated in active form. AT: "when the attackers destroy the foundations of the buildings in Egypt" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_activepassive]])\
  * **Cush and Put, Lydia and all Arabia, and Libya** - These names refer to the people of these countries. AT: "The people of Cush and Put, Libya, and all Arabia, and Lydia" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_metonymy]])
  * **Libya** - Libya is a country west of Egypt. (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:translate_names]])
  * **Lydia** - This probably refers to the kingdom of Lydia which was in what is now Turkey. 
  * **the people belonging to the covenant** - Possible meanings are 1) the "covenant" refers to the agreement that Yahweh made with the Israelites. AT: "the Jews who live in Egypt" or 2) the "covenant" refers to an agreement that Egypt made with other nations. AT: "the people of the nations that had a covenant with Egypt" or "Egypt's allies" (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_explicit]])
  * **will all fall by the sword** - Here the sword represents battle. AT: "will all die in battle" or "will all die in the war" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_metonymy]])
  * **will all fall by the sword** - The word "all" here is a generalization. It means that many people will die. (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_hyperbole]])
