===== Translation Notes: =====

  * **Oh, if only**  - This expresses great desire.
  * **there were one of you who would shut the temple gates,-**  The gates of the temple courtyard were open most of the time and were closed only by temple servants when it was necessary.
  * **so that you might not light fires on my altar in vain! -**  "so that you might not make fire to burn offerings and sin by offering worthless sacrifices." The word 'light' here refers to making a fire.
  * **accept any offering -**  "accept any sinful offering"
  * **from your hand. -**  "from you." (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_metonymy|Metonymy]])
  * **my name-**  This may be translated as "me" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_metonymy|Metonymy]]). God's name also represents his character qualities such as love and holiness.
  * **For from the rising of the sun to its setting-** This does not mean only while the sun is shining, but is another way of saying "always."  (See: [[en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_idiom|Idiom]]) 
  * **My name will be great among the nations - **"My name will be honored in the nations"
  * **in every place incense will be offered to my name, -**  "in these nations incense will be offered in worship to me." Offering incense was a way to worship God in the Old Testament.
  * **and also a pure offering. - **'pure' can mean "genuine" or "true" or "acceptable."
  * **But you are profaning it when you say the Lord's table is polluted, and its fruit, its food is to be despised. -**  "You are dishonoring my alter by saying it is dirty and the fruit and food offered on the alter are to be rejected."
