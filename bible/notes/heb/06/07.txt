===== translationNotes: =====

  * **the land that drinks in the rain** - Farmland that benefits from much rain is spoken of as if it were a person who drank in the rainwater. AT: "the land that absorbs the rain" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_personification]])
  * **that gives birth to the plants** - Farmland that produces crops is spoken of as if it gave birth to them. AT: "that produces plants" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_personification]])
  * **to those for whom the land was worked** - This can be stated in active form. AT: "to those for whom someone prepared the land" (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_activepassive]])
  * **the land that receives a blessing from God** - Rain and crops are seen as proof that God has helped the farmland. The farmland is spoken of as if it were a person who could receive God's blessing. (See: [[:en:ta:vol2:translate:figs_personification]])
  * **a blessing from God** - Here "blessing" means help from God, not spoken words. 
  * **is near to a curse** - This speaks of "curse" as if it were a place to which a person could draw near. AT: "is in danger of God cursing it"  (See: [[:en:ta:vol1:translate:figs_metaphor]])
  * **Its end is in burning** - God will cause everything on that field to burn.
