====== Personification ======


===== Description =====

Personification is giving human attributes to a non-human object (i.e. thing, idea or animal). Personification helps a writer to make something more vivid or life-like. It adds depth to writing and helps the reader 'feel' the subject matter more fully.

=== Genesis 1:18 ===

"God set them (lights) in the sky to give light upon the earth, to rule over the day and over the night, and to divide the light from the darkness."

  * **(1) to rule over the day**  - **(3)**  This phrase describes the lights as having the same authority over the day as a ruler who directs a group of people. **(4)**  This can be translated as "to direct the daytime" or "to mark the times of the day." **(5)**  (See: Personification)
=== Galatians 3:7 ===

"The scripture foresaw that God would justify the Gentiles by faith. "

  * **(1) scripture foresaw**  - **(3)**  Because God made the promise to Abraham and they wrote it down before the promise came through Christ, the scripture is like someone who knows the future before it happens. **(4)**  This can be translated as "predicted" or "saw before it happened." **(5)**  (See: Personification)
