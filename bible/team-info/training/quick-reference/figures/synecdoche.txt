====== Synecdoche ======


===== Description =====

Synecdoche is a figure of speech in which 1) the name of a part of something is used to refer to the whole thing, or 2) the name of a whole thing is used to refer to just one part of it.

===== Bible Examples =====

Field reference numbers are included for clarity purposes only. They are omitted in the actual note.

=== Genesis 22:19 ===

So Abraham returned to his young men, and they departed and went together to Beersheba, and he lived at Beersheba.

  * **(1) he lived at Beersheba**  - **(2)**  “Abraham lived at Beersheba.” **(3)**  Only Abraham was named because he was the leader of his family and servants. **(4)**  Alternate translation: “Abraham and his people stayed in Beersheba.” **(5)**  (See: Synecdoche)
=== Luke 24:16 ===

"But their eyes were prevented from recognizing him."

  * **(1) Their eyes were prevented from recognizing him**  - **(2)**  "Their eyes were kept from recognizing Jesus." **(3)**  The men's ability to recognize Jesus is spoken of as their eyes' ability to recognize him. **(4)**  Alternate translation: "They were kept from recognizing him" or "Something prevented them so they could not recognize him." **(5)**  (See: Synecdoche)
=== Philippians 2:11 ===

And every tongue should confess that Jesus Christ is Lord, to the glory of God the Father.

  * **(1) every tongue**  - **(3)**  The word “tongue” is used to refer to the whole person. **(4)**  Alternate translation: “every person” or “every being.” **(5)**  (See: Synecdoche)
