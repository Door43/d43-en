====== Adjectival Noun ======


===== Description =====

There are two types of Adjectival Nouns.

  - A noun that acts like an adjective and describes another noun.
  - An adjective that is used as a noun to refer to a category of people.

==== Adjectives as Nouns ====

=== Rut:02:20 ===

"And Naomi said to her daughter-in-law, 'May he be blessed by Yahweh, who has not left off his loyalty to the living and to the dead.'"

  * **(1) to the living and the dead**  - **(2)** "to those those who are alive and to those who have died." **(3)**  The phrase "the living" here refers to Naomi and Ruth, and "the dead" refers to their husbands. **(4)** Alternate translation: "to us who are still alive and to our husbands who have died." **(5)**  (See: Adjectival Noun)
=== Rut:01:08 ===

"May Yahweh show loyalty toward you, as you have shown loyalty toward the dead and toward me."

  * **(1) the dead**  - **(2)** "those who have died." **(3)**  This refers specifically to their husbands. **(4)**  Alternate translation: "your husbands who died." **(5)**  (See: Adjectival Noun)

=== James 4:6 ===

But God gives even more grace, which is why the scripture says, "God resists the proud, but gives grace to the humble."

  * **(1) the proud**  - **(3)**  This refers to proud people in general. **(4)** Alternate translation: "the proud person" or "proud people" or “prideful people.” **(5)**  (See: Adjectival Noun)
  * **(1) the humble**  - **(3)**  This refers to humble people in general. **(4)** Alternate translation: "the humble person" or “humble people.”
