====== Now ======

===== Description =====

The Hebrew conjunction //w// is sometimes used to introduce a topic shift or new information. The Greek //kai// and //de// are used in similar ways. Modern languages often have similar forms.
===== Standard Notes =====

In a story:

  * **Now**  - This word is used here to mark a break in the main storyline. Here [the narrator] tells background information about … (See: Background information)
  * **Now**  - This word is used here to mark a break in the main storyline. Here [the narrator] starts to tell a new part of the story.
  * **Now**  - This word is used here to mark a break in the main storyline. Here [the narrator] tells about a new person in the story.
  * **Now**  - This word is used here to mark a break in the main storyline. Here [the narrator] explains …

Not in a story:

  * **Now**  - This word is used here to mark a break in the main teaching. Here [the teacher] tells background information about … (See: Background information)
  * **Now**  - This word is used here to mark a break in the main teaching. Here [the teacher] starts to tell a new part of the teaching.
  * **Now**  - This word is used here to mark a break in the main teaching. Here [the teacher] tells about a new person in the teaching.
  * **Now**  - This word is used here to mark a break in the main teaching. Here [the teacher] explains …

When writing the note replace "[the narrator]" or "[the teacher]" with the name of the person that is using the word "now."
